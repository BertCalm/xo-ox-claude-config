# D003 — The Physics IS the Synthesis

*Established: 2026-03-14 | Seances: 9 of 24 complete*

## The Doctrine

When an engine's synthesis method is rooted in physical or mathematical rigor — real differential equations, actual Bessel functions, proper Dattorro topologies, empirically-derived bounding boxes — the rigor itself becomes the sound. Don't approximate the physics. Be the physics. The math is not overhead; the math is the instrument.

## Evidence Across Engines

- **Obscura**: Unanimous (all 8 ghosts) — "Physics IS synthesis." The physical modeling excitation/resonator architecture doesn't simulate real instruments; it IS a real physical system, just one made of code instead of wood and metal.
- **Ouroboros**: Declared "most scientifically rigorous engine in the gallery." Lorenz (1963), Rossler (1976), Chua (1983), Aizawa (1982) — each attractor cited by publication. Bounding boxes documented as "empirically derived." The Leash mechanism praised as a genuine control-theory solution to chaos taming.
- **Orbital**: "Double-precision phases exceptional." The group envelope system praised as the "crown jewel" — mathematical precision in phase accumulation creates timbral accuracy that listeners feel even if they can't name it.

## Ghosts Who Championed This

- **Don Buchla**: Electronics as art medium. The circuit IS the composition.
- **Bob Moog** (Obscura): "A well-tuned differential equation sings better than a sampled recording of singing."
- **Vangelis** (Ouroboros, Orbital): Praised the mathematical beauty as "the closest code gets to nature."

## Implications

When building physically or mathematically motivated engines:
- Cite your sources (paper, year, author) in code comments
- Use real constants, not rounded approximations
- Document where empirical values come from
- Double precision where phase accuracy matters
- The rigor is a feature — expose it in documentation and preset naming
- Don't water down the math for "usability" — build good UX around the real math instead
