# V001 — Cross-Voice Coupling is the Future of Drum Synthesis

*Recorded: 2026-03-14 | Engine: Onset | Ghost: Ikutaro Kakehashi*

## The Vision

Onset's XVC (Cross-Voice Coupling) system — where the peak amplitude of one drum voice modulates parameters of another — is "3-5 years ahead of the industry." Every drum machine treats voices as isolated channels. XVC treats them as a conversation. When the kick's transient opens the hi-hat's decay, or the snare's crack brightens the tom's resonance, the kit stops being 8 independent instruments and becomes one organism.

## Why This Matters

Traditional drum machines (TR-808, LinnDrum, Volca Drums) are 8 parallel monosynths sharing a clock. XVC introduces the concept that a drum kit's voices should influence each other the way a real kit does — sympathetic resonance, shared air, coupled membranes. This is the first time a software drum engine has formalized inter-voice modulation as a first-class feature.

## The Prophecy

Kakehashi sees XVC becoming a standard feature in next-generation drum engines across the industry. The 8x8 modulation matrix between voices is a prototype for something larger: instruments where every voice is aware of every other voice, creating emergent rhythmic behavior that no single voice could produce alone.

## What Must Happen

- XVC needs to be documented as a named concept, not just a parameter block
- Presets should showcase XVC prominently — the coupling IS the preset, not the individual voice tuning
- Onset's factory library should include "XVC showcase" presets that sound radically different from anything without coupling
