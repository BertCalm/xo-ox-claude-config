# B015 — XObese's Mojo Control

*Blessed: 2026-03-14 | Engine: XObese (FAT) | Ghosts: Moog, Buchla, Vangelis, Schulze*

## The Blessed Feature

The Mojo control is an orthogonal analog/digital character axis that operates independently from waveform shape. At Mojo=0, oscillators exhibit heavy analog drift (Perlin noise on pitch, phase, and level). At Mojo=1, oscillators are digitally precise. This creates a performance axis where *character* and *waveform* are independent controls — something no other XO_OX engine or most commercial synths offer.

## Why It Is Protected

Four ghosts independently praised the principle that character and waveform should be orthogonal. This is a design insight that should inform future XO_OX engines. The Mojo axis proves that "analog warmth" is not a binary state but a continuous, performable dimension. Vangelis compared it to the CS-80's ring modulator — a signature that separates the instrument from all others.

## Do Not

- Remove the orthogonal relationship between Mojo and waveform shape
- Make Mojo a simple "analog mode" toggle (the continuous axis IS the feature)
- Reduce the Perlin drift model to simple random noise
- Limit Mojo's influence to only the oscillator section (it should eventually permeate the entire signal chain)
