# B009 — XOverworld's ERA Triangle

*Blessed: 2026-03-14 | Engine: XOverworld | Ghosts: Buchla, Schulze, Vangelis, Pearlman*

## The Blessed Feature

The ERA (Engine-Region-Architecture) triangle is a 2D barycentric crossfade between three complete synthesis architectures — NES 2A03, Genesis YM2612, and SNES SPC700. Any point inside the triangle produces a unique timbral blend. With 6 assignable chip engines and C(6,3)=20 possible vertex configurations, the triangle contains 20 distinct timbral universes.

The ERA system includes three companion features: ERA Drift (autonomous position wandering with 4 modes), ERA Portamento (smooth position transitions), and ERA Memory (ghost layer blending present synthesis with a time-delayed position). Together, these create timbral evolution that carries its own history.

## Why It Is Protected

No other instrument offers a 2D crossfade between complete synthesis architectures. This is not a blend knob — it is a navigable timbral territory. The Drift + Memory + Portamento trio creates a form of temporal synthesis unique to XOverworld: the present moment contains an echo of the recent past in a *different timbre*. This is unprecedented.

## Do Not

- Replace with a simple crossfade slider (the 2D geometry IS the feature)
- Remove ERA Memory (the ghost layer is what separates this from a simple blend)
- Remove any of the 6 chip engines — the combinatorial space needs all vertices
- Decouple ERA Drift from the triangle — the autonomous wandering creates generative behavior
