# B013 — XOceanic's Chromatophore Modulator

*Blessed: 2026-03-14 | Engine: XOceanic | Ghosts: Buchla, Schulze, Kakehashi, Tomita*

## The Blessed Feature

The Chromatophore Modulator applies phase-separated waveforms (including a Drift mode summing three irrational-frequency sine waves) to five serial pedalboard effects simultaneously. Inspired by cuttlefish chromatophores that shift color across the animal's skin, it creates organic, never-exactly-repeating modulation patterns across the entire effects chain from a single rate control.

## Why It Is Protected

No other modulation system in any XO_OX engine (or commercial synth) uses this architecture — phase-separated, multi-target, biologically-inspired modulation. The Drift mode's three irrational-frequency ratios (0.37, 0.71, 1.13) produce patterns that don't repeat for approximately 27 seconds at the slowest rate, creating organic movement that defies the mechanical feel of synchronized LFOs. Four ghosts independently praised it as genuinely original.

## Do Not

- Replace with independent per-effect LFOs (the *unified* modulation is the concept)
- Remove the Drift mode's irrational frequency ratios
- Synchronize the phase offsets (the phase separation IS the chromatophore behavior)
- Reduce the number of modulation targets below 5
