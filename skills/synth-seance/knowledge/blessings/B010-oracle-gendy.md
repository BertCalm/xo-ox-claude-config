# B010 — Oracle's GENDY Stochastic Synthesis

*Blessed: 2026-03-14 | Engine: Oracle | Ghost: Buchla (10/10)*

## The Blessed Feature

Oracle implements Iannis Xenakis's 1991 GENDY (GENeration DYnamique) algorithm — stochastic waveform generation where breakpoints are randomly walked each cycle, producing timbres that are genuinely unpredictable and never repeat. Combined with the maqam scale quantization system, Oracle produces sounds that bridge the gap between Western experimental music and Middle Eastern/North African modal traditions.

## Why It Is Protected

Buchla gave this a 10/10 — his only perfect score across all 24 seances. GENDY is not an approximation or a "inspired by" implementation; it is the genuine Xenakis algorithm producing sounds with no acoustic precedent. The maqam system grounds these alien timbres in one of humanity's oldest musical traditions, creating a dialogue between the unprecedented and the ancient. No commercial synthesizer has combined these two ideas.

## Do Not

- Replace GENDY with a simpler noise/random source
- Remove the maqam quantization system
- "Tame" the stochastic output to make it more predictable
- Simplify the breakpoint walking algorithm
