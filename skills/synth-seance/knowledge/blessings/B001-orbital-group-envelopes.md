# B001 — Orbital's Group Envelope System

*Blessed: 2026-03-14 | Engine: Orbital | Ghost: Bob Moog, Dave Smith*

## The Blessed Feature

Orbital's group envelope system — where additive partials are grouped and share envelope contours — is the "crown jewel" of the engine. This allows timbral evolution that no individual-partial envelope system could achieve efficiently, and no subtractive filter could replicate.

## Why It Is Protected

The group envelope creates the unique "breathing" quality of Orbital's additive synthesis. Removing it, simplifying it to per-voice envelopes, or replacing it with a filter would destroy the engine's sonic identity. The double-precision phase accumulation that supports this system is not over-engineering — it is the foundation of Orbital's timbral accuracy.

## Do Not

- Replace group envelopes with per-partial envelopes (too expensive, wrong paradigm)
- Replace with subtractive filtering ("just add a filter" would destroy the additive identity)
- Reduce phase precision from double to float (audible in sustained tones)
